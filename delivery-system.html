03

Brief Template

The "no vague asks" guardrail — every deliverable starts with a short brief:

Goal What outcome this communication must drive
Audience Who needs to know / do / feel what
Msg Spine 3 key messages + proof points
Tone Confident / empathetic / urgent / celebratory
CTA What happens next — link, action, RSVP, escalation path
Risks What not to say / what must be verified
Approvers Names/roles + SLA (e.g., 24h / 48h)
04

Approval Choreography

How I prevent bottlenecks and keep things moving through the approval chain.

Approval map
Legal / Policy → Program Owner → Leadership → Final QA
Rules that keep things moving
→ One "single-thread" owner — no parallel chaos
→ Clear SLA for approvals (24–48 hours where possible)
→ Two versions only: Draft and Final — no "final_final2"
→ If approvals stall: escalate with options (Approve / Edit / Defer)
05

Quality System

My content QA checklist — before anything ships, I check:

Accuracy

Facts verified; dates/names correct; no speculation

Message Hierarchy

Headline matches body; key message is early

Audience Clarity

"What changes for me" is explicit

Tone & Voice

Consistent, human, non-defensive

Readability

Short sentences; scannable structure; active verbs

Accessibility

Inclusive language; clear labels; meaningful link text

Consistency

Aligns with the narrative platform and previous comms

06

Cadence

How I run the week — structured rhythm that keeps delivery on track.

Weekly Planning

Priorities, content calendar, dependencies, risk items

Mid-Week Check

Unblock approvals, confirm timelines, adjust scope

Post-Ship Loop

Capture learnings and update templates/playbooks

07

Measurement

How I prove effectiveness — signals I track, based on channel:

Opens / CTR (email) Engagement (intranet/social) Attendance (events) Top questions + sentiment Drop-off points Recurring confusion
Output A short "What we learned" note: What landed · what didn't · what we'll change next time.
